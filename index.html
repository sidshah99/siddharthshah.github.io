Ahiṃsā Paramo Dharma

"Non-violence is the highest path"

An open resource for understanding Jain philosophy, ethics, and history — one of the world's oldest continuously practised traditions, built on non-violence, many-sided truth, and non-attachment.

The Three Jewels

Right Faith — Samyak Darśana: A clear perception of reality free of inherited bias.

Right Knowledge — Samyak Jñāna: Understanding how karma binds and how to reverse it.

Right Conduct — Samyak Cāritra: Living ethically through vows and restraint.

The Five Great Vows

  1. Ahimsa (non-violence)
  2. Satya (truthfulness)
  3. Asteya (non-stealing)
  4. Brahmacharya (chastity)
  5. Aparigraha (non-attachment)

About the Founder

Siddharth Shah

Siddharth is a technologist and lifelong student of Jain philosophy. Growing up in a Jain family in the United States, he learned Sanskrit, studied classical Jain texts, and participated in the rituals and practices that have sustained the faith across centuries.

He built Anekant to make these teachings accessible to anyone seeking to understand the depth and nuance of Jain dharma — whether they're members of the community or curious visitors exploring the path of non-violence.

Anekant is a living resource, always open to contribution, correction, and growth. If you see something that could be clearer, more accurate, or more complete, please reach out. The work of keeping a tradition alive and relevant belongs to all of us.
